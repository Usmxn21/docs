Revtain is a payment recovery engine. When a charge fails on your gateway, Revtain runs intelligent retries with prediction-driven timing, prioritising recovery on the original gateway and routing to backup gateways for re-collected cards. Your checkout doesn't change. Your customers don't see anything different. You receive a webhook when the payment is recovered.

These docs cover everything you need to integrate Revtain — whether you're using a supported billing platform (no code) or wiring it up to your own.

## Integration Tiers

Revtain has two integration tiers. Most established subscription businesses use FAST_TRACK; greenfield deployments and new product lines use ENGINE_ROOM. The two are often combined — ENGINE_ROOM for new customer signups going forward, FAST_TRACK for the existing customer base.

| Tier | Best for | What you get | What you do |
|------|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **FAST_TRACK** | Established subscription businesses with existing customers vaulted in their primary gateway | Same-gateway intelligent retries on Day 1 across the entire customer base. Cross-gateway recovery emerges progressively as customers re-collect cards through Revtain's hosted card update flow. | Send Revtain failed payment webhooks. Optionally configure backup gateways during onboarding for the cross-gateway upgrade path. |
| **ENGINE_ROOM** | Greenfield deployments, new product lines, customer bases captured going forward | Cross-gateway recovery from the first transaction for customers captured via Revtain's secure card collection iFrame. Applies to new signups post-integration; pre-existing cards in the merchant's prior gateway remain on FAST_TRACK paths. | One-time checkout integration via Revtain's iFrame. See the [ENGINE_ROOM iFrame Integration](/guides/iframe-integration) guide. |

## Base URL

```
https://api.revtain.com
```

Same URL for testing and production. Test mode is controlled by your gateway type — onboard with a test gateway (e.g., Stripe `sk_test_...`) for sandbox, live key for production.
